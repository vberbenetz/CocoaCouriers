Why Fair Trade?

Fair Pay

For many years large chocolate corporations have been taking advantage of the cacao farmers in developing countries. Bean-to-bar means that chocolate makers are buying directly from the farmers fair prices and cutting out the middle man. This makes for better and healthier working conditions where farmers have control over the way their farms are run.

Sustainable Trade

Dealing with farmers and paying higher prices means that the product is grown in a more natural way without the need for genetically modified trees. Paying fair prices for product allows the farmer to focus on producing the finest quality product rather than the highest quantity.

Sustainable Local Economies

By buying fair trade, we are keeping money in the economies that are producing our product. This promotes growth of small business and development of less privileged nations
