Dark Chocolate is an Antioxidant Powerhouse!

  • Feb 5, 2016 - Joseph Grande - Co-Founder | CocoaCouriers
  • Share

Did you know that Dark chocolate is actually really high in antioxidants? The way that researches measure the amount of antioxidants that a food contains is by using a measurement called ORAC. ORAC stands for Oxygen Radical Absorbance Capacity. Researchers take the food in question and put it up against free radicals (these are the bad molecules in your body which can harm the health of an organism)

The price of cacao (the source of all thigs chocolate) has been rising steadily in international markets, some large manufacturers have combatted the rise in prices by changing their recipes to make smaller treats or adding more milk.

Yield Issues

Did you know all of the world’s cacao (the magical chocolate bearing fruit) is grown within 20 degrees of the equator? This is the only climate that supports the growth of temperamental cacao trees! So farmers in this part of the world are extremely susceptible to crop destroying issues like drought and disease such as Witches’ Broom (Moniliophthora perniciosa) which had a devastating impact on Brazil’s crops in the region of Bahia for the past 10 years where production was reduced by 70%.

Increased Demand

Has your taste for chocolate matured since you were 6? Ours has, and world trends are showing we’re not the only ones. With a higher demand for richer flavors and higher percentages in our chocolates we consume more cocoa.

Increased Chinese Buying Power

The middle class in China has been growing quite quickly, today they are now seen as the biggest middle class in the world and are growing at a faster rate than America’s according to research by Credit Suisse. Their changing tastes also include fine chocolate which has now become affordable for millions more in this growing economy.

With such a huge economy adding demand to the cocoa industry the prices have continued to rise and will continue to do so in the future.

However this is no call for alarm or panic if you feel like you can’t live without chocolate. Commodities typically balance their selves out as the price of them rise, the demand to purchase them at the higher price lowers, and the market adjusts for the change in purchasing habits.
